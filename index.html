ORIGIN & HISTORY

  • Rock music began in the United States in the late 1940s and early 1950s, evolving from rock and roll.
  • It was influenced by other genres like blues, country, gospel, and R&B.
  • After World War II, American society changed — young people had more freedom, and the radio and television spread new music fast. Rock became the voice of youth and rebellion.
  • The pioneers of rock included Chuck Berry, Little Richard, and Elvis Presley — they shaped the sound of early rock with electric guitar riffs and energetic performances.
  • In the 1960s, rock spread across the world, especially through the British Invasion with bands like The Beatles and The Rolling Stones.
